**Beyond Orbit** is a persistent, browser-based sci-fi simulation set about a hundred years from now. No aliens. Realistic physics. The technology people rely on is mostly still experimental. Humanity has only recently pushed past Sol into the neighboring stars, and the universe keeps moving whether you're logged in or not.

It's an asynchronous idle game: always on, played in sessions of 10–30 minutes a few times a day. Actions take time. What comes out of them depends on your skills and choices, and what the rest of the world was doing while you were away.

---

### The setting

The solar system is settled. Alpha Centauri and Gliese are early in colonization. Everything beyond that is unmapped and waiting. FTL travel exists, but jump gates are experimental, half-tested, and expensive. Most of the technology people depend on breaks more often than it should.

Water and ice are worth more than they look. Raw resources are plentiful, but moving, refining, and guarding them is where the actual money is.

---

### How it works

Beyond Orbit is menu-based. You're given situations with options shaped by your skills, perks, resources, and current context: mining an asteroid, negotiating a deal, slipping past a patrol, surviving an ambush. Some you walk into. Others get forced on you.

You decide what to pursue, what to build, what to risk. Every action costs time. Outcomes depend on how well you prepared.

Skills go up with use and decay when ignored. When they decay, you lose efficiency. The capability stays. You can't master everything, so the players who do best are the ones who specialise.

---

### The universe is shared

Players interact through a live economy, shared outcomes, and events with competitive goals. Safe zones cover most of settled space. Outside them you're hard to find, but if someone does find you, you're also harder to keep alive.

Death is expensive but rarely final. Most losses can be clawed back. Insurance softens the blow but doesn't make it free. What you build outlives any single character.
